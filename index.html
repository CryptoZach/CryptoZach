Digital money & tokenization — policy-to-implementation

Digital money and tokenization research for real‑world policy and operating constraints.

I’m Zach Zukowski (CryptoZach)—a research and strategy leader at the intersection of private digital money and core financial infrastructure: stablecoins, tokenized deposits and cash‑management products, settlement and custody plumbing, and the stability and consumer‑outcome channels that emerge as these systems scale.

My method is evidence‑first. I translate technical and policy design choices into scenario trees with monitoring thresholds and escalation pathways (what triggers a memo, a data request, supervisory questions, or a policy option set).

The goal: steward infrastructure that is safe under stress, measurable in operation, and aligned with the public interest—modernizing money and settlement without creating opaque choke points.

What I work on

Four recurring problem families where design choices become measurable stability, supervision, payments, and consumer outcomes.

Private money, bank funding, and credit migration

Stablecoins, tokenized deposits, and tokenized cash‑management products—liability design, reserve custody/composition, redemption operations, and how balances and credit migrate across bank and nonbank balance sheets under adoption and stress.

Settlement & market plumbing

Settlement finality, intraday liquidity mobilization, custody/segregation and reconciliation, and the short‑term funding market plumbing that becomes binding as tokenized instruments scale.

The control layer

Where policy becomes operational reality: screening, holds, routing defaults, disclosures, and recordkeeping. Focus areas include concentration risk, third‑party dependency, failure‑mode testing, and rules‑based due‑process operations with measurable outcomes.

Governance, incentives & stress dynamics

Run dynamics, incentive misalignment, and operational/cyber concentration. I translate mechanisms into scenarios, thresholds, and escalation playbooks that keep views current as conditions change.
