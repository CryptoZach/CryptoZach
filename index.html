Digital money and tokenization research for real‑world policy and operating constraints.

I’m Zach Zukowski (CryptoZach)—a research and strategy leader at the intersection of private digital money and core financial infrastructure: stablecoins, tokenized deposits/cash‑management products, settlement and custody plumbing, and the stability and consumer‑outcome channels that emerge as these systems scale.

My method is evidence‑first. I translate technical and policy design choices into scenario trees with monitoring thresholds and escalation pathways (what triggers a memo, a data request, supervisory questions, or a policy option set).

The goal: build and steward infrastructure that is safe under stress, measurable in operation, and aligned with the public interest—modernizing money and settlement without creating opaque choke points.

Operating model

Director‑level research leadership bridging private money and financial infrastructure: payments safety/efficiency, bank funding & liquidity channels, operational resilience, and financial stability.

  • Research OS: agenda‑setting, QA standards, publication cadence, decision logs
  • Measurement layer: redemption latency/reliability, settlement exceptions, intraday liquidity constraints
  • Control‑layer metrics: screening/holds/routing/disclosures/recordkeeping + concentration/third‑party dependency
  • Consumer outcomes: error‑resolution timelines, complaint/appeal throughput, outcome reporting for restrictions/holds

Prefer: measurable guardrails + explicit tradeoffs. Avoid: hype, advocacy, and category errors.

What I work on

Private money, bank funding, and credit migration

Stablecoins, tokenized deposits, and tokenized cash‑management products—liability design, reserve custody/composition, redemption operations, and how balances and credit migrate across bank and nonbank balance sheets under adoption and stress.

Settlement & market plumbing

Settlement finality, intraday liquidity mobilization, custody/segregation and reconciliation, and the short‑term funding market plumbing that becomes binding as tokenized instruments scale.

The control layer

Where policy becomes operational reality: screening, holds, routing defaults, disclosures, and recordkeeping. Focus areas include concentration risk, third‑party dependency, failure‑mode testing, and due‑process operations with measurable outcomes.

Governance, incentives & stress dynamics

Run dynamics, incentive misalignment, and operational/cyber concentration. I translate mechanisms into scenarios, thresholds, and escalation playbooks that keep views current as conditions change.

Reusable artifacts

I tend to produce reusable artifacts—dashboards, decision logs, and concise memos—that make assumptions reviewable and keep stakeholders aligned.

  • Research agendas with a clear question hierarchy: what must be true, what can be uncertain, and what is worth monitoring.
  • Scenario trees with thresholds and escalation pathways (memo → data request → supervisory questions → policy options).
  • Control‑layer metrics dashboards for screening/holds/routing/disclosures/recordkeeping, including concentration metrics and failure‑mode testing.
  • “What would change the view” registers and decision logs that preserve institutional memory and reduce re‑litigation of assumptions.

Want something concrete? If you share the decision context (policy question, product design, or infrastructure project), I can outline:

  • the scenario tree,
  • the minimum viable metric set,
  • the escalation workflow,
  • and the “what changes the view” triggers.
